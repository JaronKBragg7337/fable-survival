# PLAYER_FEEDBACK.md — What Players Experienced

A changelog records what we changed. This records what players felt.
Agents: read this when picking roadmap work — patterns here outrank assumptions.
After a playtest, add an entry at the top and translate real signals into
ROADMAP.md items.

## Privacy rules (non-negotiable)

- **Never log a player's real name** unless they are on the Dev Team list below.
- Everyone else is identified by a **handle they chose themselves** (gamertag,
  nickname) or a generic label like "Tester #3 (phone)". If a chosen handle
  happens to be their real name, that was their choice and it's fine.
- Most testers are kids (owner's son's friends). Log **no** personal details:
  no ages, schools, locations, contact info, or social accounts. Gameplay
  observations only.
- If feedback arrives via screenshot/chat, extract the gameplay content; don't
  paste identifying material into this file.

## YYYY-MM-DD — <handle or Dev Team name> — <platform>

## 2026-07-02 — Kurtis (Dev Team) — phone

**Raw feedback:** "I died by zombies again. It is a really good game though,
I like it. It's a good version of it right now. Can't wait to see the full
version." Died to zombies multiple times across sessions.

**Observations:**
- First-time player onboarded with zero help — controls and goal were clear
  enough that his feedback was about *gameplay*, not confusion or bugs. The
  foundation is invisible, which is what it should be.
- Repeated zombie deaths + still enjoying it = difficulty is engaging, but
  we're near the line. Watch whether other testers bounce off it.
- "Can't wait for the full version" = appetite for content > polish right now.

**Action items (filed to ROADMAP.md):**
- Difficulty tuning/settings (Milestone 1 addition)
- Better zombie audio/visual cues before they hit you (folds into the
  Milestone 1 sound item)
- Consider a gentle first-day grace period (fewer zombies near spawn on Day 1)
